Spatial Distribution Analysis

Villages, Centers, Road Network, Public Services & Nile Islands
Assiut Governorate – Egypt

Project Overview

This project analyzes the spatial distribution of villages and administrative centers in Assiut Governorate, in relation to road networks, public services, and Nile River islands. The objective is to understand spatial patterns, accessibility, and service coverage to support regional and urban planning.

Data Used

  • Administrative Boundaries (Governorate, Centers, Villages)
  • Road Network (Primary & Secondary Roads)
  • Public Services (Health, Education, Government Facilities)
  • Nile River and Nile Islands
  • Satellite Imagery

Tools & Software

ArcGIS Pro Spatial Analyst QGIS Remote Sensing

Data Sources

  • Egyptian General Authority for Survey (EGAS)
  • Central Agency for Public Mobilization and Statistics (CAPMAS)
  • OpenStreetMap (Roads & Services)
  • USGS Earth Explorer (Satellite Imagery)
  • NASA Earth Data (Remote Sensing Data)
  • Humanitarian Data Exchange (HDX)

Methodology

  • Collecting and preparing spatial datasets from multiple sources
  • Georeferencing satellite imagery
  • Digitizing villages, centers, roads, services, and Nile islands
  • Spatial distribution and proximity analysis
  • Overlay analysis between services, roads, and settlements
  • Cartographic map design and visualization

Final Maps

Results & Conclusion

The results demonstrate the spatial distribution patterns of villages and administrative centers in Assiut Governorate and their connectivity through the road network. The analysis highlights service coverage gaps and the spatial importance of Nile islands, providing valuable insights for infrastructure development and service planning.
